Description of Newlang

A full description of Newlang is as follows.

Morphology and Syntax

Newlang is full of inflections that permit some freedom in word order but the default structure of Newlang is head final, subject-verb-object. Newlang is an agglutinating language. Genitives follow nouns and the word de, adpositions are called prepositions because they precede their clauses, and nouns must precede their relative clauses. Finally, even though Newlang is head-final, adverbs follow their verbs.

The red bird quickly went to the dog's house.

would be

The red bird went quickly to house of the dog.

If you are doing a language sketch, then this may be enough. Proceed with caution.

Nouns

Newlang nouns inflect for number, case, gender, possesion, ...

Noun cases are: actor, patient, or broker and these functions/cases are identified by adding/changing/removing ...

Noun numbers are: singular, dual, plural, non-zero, fractional, ...

Noun genders are: animate, inanimate, fey, ...

Adjectives

Newlang adjectives (dis)agree with their nouns but only in case and number. They are further inflected for evidence.

Because of the agreement with their nouns, adjectives can come before or after but they are most often placed before. Adjectives must come before the nouns they modify. If there are multiple adjectives they are placed in order of place, time, and color.

Adjective numbers are: singular and plural. If the attached noun is dual, the adjective is marked for singular.

Adjective evidences are: seen and heard.

Demonstratives

All Newlang demonstratives are deictic and are inflected only for gender.

  • tie means that place (there) and is inherently inanimate.

  • tiu means that animate thing.

  • tius means that inanimate thing.

Verbs

Newlang verbs conjugate for tense and believability and must (dis)agree with their adverbs. Copulas are formed by using an adjective as the object of the present tense of the verb esti; la birdo estas ruja means "the bird is blue". Negation is handled by replacing all the vowels with a so la birdo ne astas ruja means "the bird isn't blue".

The tenses are: past, present, future, unlikely, by dictate of the gods, ... Past tense is created by adding t before the verb.

Adverbs

Adverbs must follow the verb they modify and are of three types: manner, location, and time.

Manner adverbs are formed by taking an adjective and inflecting it with a following e: mi iris rapide "I went quickly".

Time adverbs are unique words:

  • a means long time ago.

Participles

Participles come in two basic forms: active, meaning that the subject is doing the verb that the participle is based on; passive, meaning that the subject is having the verb done to them that the participle is based on.

Both types of participles inflect for tense and number: vidinta means "was seeing", ...
